Data Analytics & Business Intelligence (BI) Services

A Strategic Service Line for Phipps Henson McAllister

1. Introduction

In an increasingly data-driven world, the ability to extract meaningful insights from financial and operational data is no longer a luxury but a necessity for business success. Many UK SMEs are accumulating vast amounts of data through their accounting systems, CRMs, and operational platforms, yet often lack the tools, expertise, or time to transform this raw data into actionable intelligence.

Data Analytics and Business Intelligence (BI) Services empower businesses by collecting, cleaning, analyzing, and visualizing their data to reveal trends, monitor Key Performance Indicators (KPIs), forecast future performance, and support strategic decision-making. For Phipps Henson McAllister, with our unique blend of senior software engineering talent and deep accounting domain expertise, offering BI services is a logical progression. We can help clients move beyond standard financial reports to truly understand the drivers of their business performance.

This service involves creating custom interactive dashboards, developing financial forecasting models, performing profitability analysis, and structuring data for effective analysis, ultimately enabling clients to make more informed, data-backed decisions.

2. SWOT Analysis

Strengths Weaknesses
  • Synergistic Expertise: Software engineering skills are crucial for data handling, BI tool customization, and integration; accounting knowledge ensures financial data is interpreted correctly and KPIs are relevant.
  • Access to Financial Data: As existing accountants/advisors for many clients, we have trusted access to their core financial data, a key input for BI.
  • Contextual Understanding: Ability to understand the business context behind the numbers, leading to more insightful analysis than a generic BI consultant.
  • Custom Dashboard Capability: Software engineers can build highly customized dashboards beyond the limitations of standard BI tool templates if needed.
  • Trusted Advisory Role: Builds on existing client trust to offer higher-value advisory services.
  • Specialized BI Skillset: Requires dedicated expertise in specific BI platforms (Power BI, Tableau), data warehousing, and statistical analysis, which may require focused upskilling beyond general software engineering.
  • Data Quality Dependence: The adage "garbage in, garbage out" is highly relevant; BI outputs are only as good as the underlying client data quality.
  • Potentially Longer Sales Cycle: Communicating the value of BI and justifying its cost can be more complex than for more tangible services.
  • Competition from BI Specialists: Competing with dedicated BI consulting firms and data analytics specialists.
Opportunities Threats
  • Growing Market: The UK data analytics market is booming, with increasing BI adoption across industries. Gartner projects continued growth.
  • Demand for Insight: SMEs recognize the need for better insights but often lack capacity. Research indicates many SMEs only analyze "a fraction" of their data.
  • Accountant Skill Evolution: Kaplan notes "data analytics and data science skills are showing the biggest growth in demand" for finance professionals.
  • High-Value Service: BI services command premium pricing and can significantly enhance client advisory relationships.
  • Data from Digitalization: Initiatives like Making Tax Digital (MTD) mean more structured digital data is available for analysis.
  • Cross-Selling from App Advisory/Automation: Clients using well-structured apps and automated processes generate better data, creating a natural pathway to BI services.
  • Data Security and Privacy: Handling sensitive financial and operational data requires stringent security measures and GDPR compliance.
  • Complexity of Client Systems: Integrating data from disparate or legacy client systems can be challenging.
  • Client Skill Gap: Clients may lack the understanding to fully utilize or act upon the BI insights provided, requiring education.
  • Tooling Costs & Licensing: Costs associated with BI software licenses (for the firm and potentially for clients) need to be managed.
  • Keeping Pace with BI Technology: BI tools and techniques evolve rapidly, necessitating ongoing learning.

3. Learning Curve for Team

While our software engineers have strong data handling and programming skills, specialized knowledge in BI tools, data visualization, and analytical methodologies is key:

  • BI Platforms & Tools:
    • In-depth proficiency and potential certification in leading BI platforms, primarily Microsoft Power BI (e.g., Microsoft Certified: Data Analyst Associate – DA-100/PL-300) and potentially Tableau Desktop.
    • Familiarity with how these tools connect to various data sources, especially accounting software (Xero, QuickBooks, Sage).
    • ICAEW offers an "Analytics in Power BI" certificate focusing on forecasting/reporting.
  • Data Modeling & Warehousing Concepts:
    • Understanding principles of data modeling for analytical purposes (e.g., star schemas).
    • Basic knowledge of data warehousing concepts and ETL (Extract, Transform, Load) processes (e.g., using SQL, Azure Data Factory, or Python libraries).
  • Data Visualization Best Practices:
    • Skills in designing effective, clear, and actionable dashboards and reports.
    • Understanding how to choose the right chart types to convey specific insights.
  • Statistical Analysis & Financial Modeling:
    • Foundational knowledge of statistical concepts relevant to business analysis (e.g., trend analysis, variance analysis).
    • Ability to develop financial forecasting models, perform KPI calculations, and conduct profitability analysis (e.g., client, product, service). Programming in Python/R for data analysis (pandas, numpy) can be beneficial.
  • Domain-Specific KPIs & Metrics:
    • Leveraging existing accounting knowledge to define and track relevant financial and operational KPIs for different industries and business models.

Effort Estimation: Research suggests that achieving proficiency in a BI platform like Power BI (e.g., Microsoft DA-100/PL-300 exam prep) can take 2-4 weeks of intensive study and practice. Building broader expertise in data modeling, advanced analytics, and specific financial modeling techniques would be an ongoing development process, with initial projects focusing on core dashboarding and KPI reporting.

4. Ease of Launch / "Quick Win" Potential

Data Analytics & BI Services are a Strategic, Higher-Value Service rather than an immediate "quick win" in the simplest sense. However, initial "quick win" *elements* can be introduced.

  • Foundation Building: Success in BI often builds upon well-organized data from services like App Advisory (ensuring good apps are in place) and Automation (ensuring data flows efficiently).
  • Longer Development & Sales Cycle: Developing comprehensive BI solutions and educating clients on their value typically takes more time than basic app setup.
  • Initial "Quick Win" BI Offerings (Scoped):
    • "KPI Snapshot Dashboard": Create a simple, focused dashboard in Power BI (or even advanced Excel) tracking 3-5 key financial KPIs sourced directly from the client's existing cloud accounting software. This demonstrates immediate visual value.
    • "Data Health Check & BI Readiness Assessment": A service to review a client's existing data sources and practices, providing a report on their readiness for more advanced BI and identifying low-hanging fruit for data improvement.
  • Leveraging Software Engineering Skills: Our team can quickly learn to connect to data sources and build initial dashboards, especially with modern, user-friendly BI tools.

Full-scale BI with deep predictive analytics is a longer-term goal. The "quick win" lies in delivering immediate, tangible insights through basic, well-designed dashboards for existing clients eager for better visibility.

5. Pricing Strategy

Pricing for Data Analytics & BI Services should reflect the expertise involved and the significant strategic value delivered:

  • Hourly Consulting Rates:
    • For data discovery, requirements gathering, ad-hoc analysis, and ongoing support.
    • Suggested Rate: £60 - £120+ per hour. UK median contract rates for "Data Consultants" were around £581/day (approx. £70-£75/hr), with more specialized BI skills potentially commanding higher rates.
  • Fixed-Price "Starter" Packages:
    • For initial, well-scoped BI offerings aimed at demonstrating value.
    • Example: "KPI Snapshot Dashboard for [Xero/QBO]": £750 - £1,500 (covering connection to accounting data, development of a dashboard with 3-5 core KPIs, and a walkthrough session).
    • Example: "BI Readiness Assessment & Data Quality Review": £500 - £1,000.
  • Project-Based Fees (for Custom Solutions):
    • For developing comprehensive custom dashboards, data integration solutions, and advanced analytical models.
    • Pricing based on scope, complexity, number of data sources, and development time. Projects could range from £3,000 - £15,000+. Research indicates dashboard suite projects can be £5k-£30k.
  • Ongoing Analytics & Reporting Retainer:
    • For providing regular updates to dashboards, generating monthly insight reports, and offering ongoing analytical support.
    • E.g., £500 - £3,000+ per month, depending on the complexity and frequency of reporting and support.

Note: Clearly articulate the value proposition, focusing on how BI can lead to improved decision-making, efficiency gains, and ultimately, enhanced profitability. Case studies and ROI examples will be crucial.

6. Steps to Launch & Secure First Customer

Launching BI services effectively requires a phased approach, starting with foundational skills and targeted initial offerings:

  1. Core Team Upskilling & Tool Mastery (Target: 2-4 Weeks):
    • Select 1-2 software engineers with a strong aptitude for data and analytical thinking.
    • Prioritize intensive training and certification in Microsoft Power BI (e.g., PL-300). This includes data connection, transformation (Power Query), DAX for calculations, and visualization.
    • Develop internal proficiency in connecting Power BI securely to key cloud accounting platforms (Xero, QuickBooks Online, Sage Cloud Accounting).
    • Create a standardized internal template for a basic "KPI Snapshot Dashboard" that can be quickly adapted for different clients.
  2. Define Initial, Scoped BI "Taster" Offerings (Target: Concurrent with Step 1):
    • Based on the "KPI Snapshot Dashboard" concept, define a clear package. Example:
      • "SME Financial Performance Dashboard (Xero/QBO Edition)": Connects to client's Xero/QBO, visualizes 5 core financial KPIs (e.g., Revenue Trend, Gross Profit Margin, Net Profit, Debtor Days, Cash Balance Trend) with basic drill-down. Includes a 1-hour setup and 1-hour walkthrough. Launch Price: £499 - £799.
    • Also consider a "BI Readiness Assessment" package as a low-commitment entry point.
    • Focus these initial offers on leveraging data already available in the client's primary accounting system to minimize data integration complexity.
  3. Identify Pilot Candidates from Existing Engaged Clients (Target: Week 3-4):
    • Review Phipps Henson McAllister's existing client base, particularly those for whom you already provide App Advisory or have helped streamline data through automation. These clients will have better quality data.
    • Look for clients who:
      • Have expressed a desire for better financial visibility or "understanding their numbers."
      • Are proactive and interested in leveraging technology for growth.
      • Are using cloud accounting platforms where data access is straightforward.
    • Select 2-3 "innovation-friendly" clients for a pilot BI engagement.
  4. Develop Simple, Visual Marketing Materials (Target: Week 4):
    • Create a one-page PDF or a section on your website showcasing the "SME Financial Performance Dashboard" (using anonymized sample visuals).
    • Highlight key benefits: "See your key numbers at a glance," "Track performance against goals," "Make data-driven decisions faster."
    • Emphasize that this service is delivered by professionals who understand both technology and accounting.
  5. Offer Pilot Engagements to Selected Clients (Target: Week 5 onwards):
    • Approach the selected pilot clients. Offer the "SME Financial Performance Dashboard" (or similar initial package) at an attractive introductory or pilot rate.
    • Frame it as a collaborative effort: "We're expanding our services to offer powerful data insights and would value your participation and feedback as we roll out our new BI dashboards. This will give you [specific benefits] at a significantly reduced pilot fee."
    • Focus on their specific needs: "What are the 3-5 numbers you wish you could see updated daily/weekly to run your business better?"
  6. Deliver Pilot Dashboards, Gather Feedback, and Iterate (Target: Weeks 6-8):
    • Build and deliver the initial dashboards for the pilot clients.
    • Conduct thorough walkthrough sessions, explaining how to interpret the data and use the dashboard.
    • Systematically collect feedback on the dashboard's utility, clarity, ease of use, and what additional insights they would find valuable. This is crucial for refining your standard offering.
    • Document the process and any quantifiable benefits or positive client reactions (for future case studies).
  7. Refine Offering and Plan Broader Rollout (Target: Post-Pilot):
    • Based on pilot feedback, refine the standard "starter" BI packages, templates, and pricing.
    • Develop case studies from successful pilots.
    • Begin marketing the refined BI services more broadly to your client base and through targeted channels (e.g., content marketing on "Using Data in Your SME," LinkedIn showcasing dashboard examples).
