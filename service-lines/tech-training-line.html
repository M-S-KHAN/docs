Technology Upskilling & Training for Accountants

A Strategic Service Line for Phipps Henson McAllister

1. Introduction

The accountancy profession is undergoing a significant transformation driven by rapid technological advancements. Cloud accounting, automation, data analytics, and AI are reshaping traditional roles and creating a demand for new skillsets. Many accountants and finance teams, particularly within SMEs and smaller practices, recognize the need to upskill but often lack the time, resources, or specific guidance to do so effectively.

Technology Upskilling and Training for Accountants aims to bridge this skills gap by providing targeted, practical training programs designed by professionals who understand both technology and the intricacies of accounting. For Phipps Henson McAllister, with our in-house team of software engineers possessing deep accounting domain knowledge, this service allows us to empower our clients and other accounting professionals to leverage technology confidently and competently.

This service focuses on developing and delivering training on modern accounting software ecosystems, data analysis tools (like advanced Excel or Power BI basics), automation concepts, cybersecurity best practices, and other emerging technologies relevant to the modern accountant.

2. SWOT Analysis

Strengths Weaknesses
  • Unique Instructor Profile: Training delivered by software engineers who are also deeply familiar with accounting principles and workflows, providing highly relevant and practical insights.
  • Real-World Application Focus: Ability to design training content using real-world accounting scenarios and case studies.
  • Existing Client Relationships: Opportunity to offer training to current SME clients' finance staff and potentially to other local accountancy firms.
  • Credibility & Trust: As an established accountancy firm, Phipps Henson McAllister brings inherent trust and understanding of professional standards.
  • Customizable Content: Ability to tailor training programs to specific client needs or industry focuses.
  • Instructional Design Expertise: While technically proficient, the team may need to develop skills in adult learning principles, curriculum development, and engaging training delivery.
  • Marketing Training Services: Reaching and convincing potential trainees requires different marketing approaches than traditional accounting services.
  • Keeping Content Current: Technology evolves rapidly, requiring continuous updates to training materials and instructor knowledge.
  • Competition from Established Trainers: Competing with professional training companies (Kaplan, BPP) and software vendors who offer their own training.
Opportunities Threats
  • Significant Skills Gap: A PwC survey found 40% of UK firms struggle to hire necessary tech-savvy staff. QuickBooks found 85% of accountants believe willingness to learn new tech is as important as traditional skills.
  • High Demand for Training: 65% of UK Chartered Accountants expect professional bodies to provide AI/data training, indicating strong willingness and demand.
  • CPD Market: Accountants require Continuing Professional Development (CPD) hours, and accredited tech training can fulfill this need.
  • Differentiation for Accountants: Offering training can position Phipps Henson McAllister as a thought leader and attract clients seeking a tech-forward firm.
  • Partnership Opportunities: Collaborating with professional bodies (ICAEW, ACCA, AAT), software vendors, or local colleges.
  • Higher-Value Service Perception: Moving beyond compliance to empowering clients and peers with new skills.
  • Time Constraints of Accountants: Busy professionals may find it difficult to allocate time for training.
  • Cost Sensitivity: Smaller firms or individual accountants might be price-sensitive regarding training fees.
  • Generic Online Courses: Proliferation of free or low-cost generic online tech courses can be perceived as an alternative.
  • Maintaining Quality at Scale: Ensuring consistent training quality if the service grows significantly.
  • Accreditation Hurdles: Gaining official accreditation for courses from professional bodies can be time-consuming.

3. Learning Curve for Team (as Trainers)

Our software engineers and accounting professionals possess the core technical and domain knowledge. The "learning curve" is primarily in transforming this expertise into effective training delivery:

  • Instructional Design & Curriculum Development:
    • Learning how to structure effective training sessions, define learning objectives, create engaging content (slides, exercises, case studies), and develop assessment methods.
  • Adult Learning Principles:
    • Understanding how adults learn best (e.g., experiential learning, problem-based learning) and adapting teaching styles accordingly.
  • Training Delivery & Facilitation Skills:
    • Developing skills in public speaking, managing group dynamics, facilitating discussions, answering questions effectively, and using training technologies (e.g., webinar platforms, LMS).
  • Specific Software Certification for Training (if required):
    • Some software vendors (e.g., Xero "Train the Trainer" or similar) might offer programs for those wishing to deliver official certified training on their platforms.
  • Familiarity with CPD Requirements:
    • Understanding the CPD requirements of major UK accounting bodies (ICAEW, ACCA, AAT, CIMA) to align course content and potentially seek accreditation.

Effort Estimation: Acquiring foundational training delivery skills could involve short courses or workshops (e.g., a "Train the Trainer" course might be 2-3 days). Developing a specific curriculum for a new topic might take 1-2 weeks of dedicated effort for the first iteration. The key is practical application and refinement based on initial training sessions. Ongoing improvement is iterative.

4. Ease of Launch / "Quick Win" Potential

Technology Upskilling and Training can be a "Quick Win" IF focused on leveraging Phipps Henson McAllister's existing, demonstrable areas of deep expertise.

  • Leverage In-House Mastery: Identify specific tech tools or processes where our software engineers or tech-savvy accountants already possess advanced skills that are in demand (e.g., advanced Excel for financial modeling, deep practical use of Xero/QBO beyond basics, Python for basic data tasks).
  • Start with Short, Focused Workshops/Webinars: Instead of developing extensive courses initially, offer:
    • 2-3 hour hands-on workshops.
    • 60-90 minute "lunch and learn" webinars.
  • Target Existing Clients & Local Networks: Promote these initial, focused training sessions to the finance staff of our current SME clients and to other local Northampton/Midlands accountancy firms.
  • Minimal Curriculum Development for Core Expertise: If teaching what the team already knows deeply, initial curriculum development is about structuring that knowledge for delivery, not learning new content from scratch.
  • Directly Addresses Known Pain Points: Training on topics that solve immediate efficiency or capability issues for accountants will see quicker uptake.

Not a Quick Win If: Attempting to immediately launch a broad catalogue of accredited courses on unfamiliar technologies. This requires significant curriculum development, accreditation processes, and potentially hiring specialist trainers.

5. Pricing Strategy

Pricing for Technology Upskilling and Training should be competitive yet reflect the specialized knowledge and practical value delivered:

  • Per Participant for Workshops/Webinars:
    • Common for shorter, open-enrollment sessions.
    • Example: 2-3 hour Webinar: £75 - £150 per participant.
    • Example: Half-day Workshop (3-4 hours): £150 - £300 per participant.
    • Example: Full-day Workshop (6-7 hours): £300 - £600+ per participant. Research indicates typical corporate training can be £300-£1,000/person-day.
  • Corporate / In-House Training (Group Rates):
    • For delivering training exclusively to a team within a single firm/company.
    • Often priced as a day rate for the trainer plus materials, or a fixed fee for a set number of participants.
    • Example: Trainer Day Rate: £700 - £1,500+ depending on expertise and content complexity. (Research suggests £500-£1,000/day with minimums).
  • Packaged Courses / Series:
    • For a series of linked workshops or a more comprehensive certificate-style program (if developed later).
    • E.g., A "Digital Accountant Toolkit" series of 3 half-day workshops could be £400 - £750 per participant for the series.
  • Online Self-Paced Courses (Future Potential):
    • If content is recorded and hosted on an LMS. Typically lower price point per course (e.g., £50 - £200), but requires volume.

Note: Offering early bird discounts, group booking rates, or bundled packages (e.g., training + a short advisory session) can incentivize uptake. Highlighting if courses contribute to CPD hours is a strong selling point. Market research shows ICAEW certificate courses can cost around £700.

6. Steps to Launch & Secure First Trainee/Client

To launch Technology Upskilling and Training, focusing on leveraging existing expertise for "quick wins":

  1. Identify Core In-House Expertise for Initial Training Topics (Target: 1 Week):
    • Survey Phipps Henson McAllister's software engineers and tech-savvy accounting staff: What specific software, tools, or tech-driven accounting processes do they master and could confidently teach *right now* with minimal preparation?
      • Examples: Advanced Excel for financial reporting, practical Xero/QBO power-user tips, automating tasks with basic Power Automate flows, cybersecurity best practices for client data.
    • Select 1-2 initial topics that are highly practical, address common accountant pain points, and require the least new curriculum development.
  2. Develop Lean Training Materials for Chosen Topic(s) (Target: 1-2 Weeks):
    • For the selected initial topic(s), create a concise set of materials:
      • A structured outline / agenda (e.g., for a 2-hour webinar or 3-hour workshop).
      • Key talking points and simple slides (if applicable).
      • 1-2 practical exercises or real-world examples that participants can work through.
    • Focus on practical application, not theoretical lectures. The goal is "actionable takeaways."
  3. Define & Price an Attractive Introductory Training Offer (Target: Concurrent with Step 2):
    • Based on the initial topic and format (e.g., webinar, short workshop), set an introductory price.
      • Example: "90-Minute Power Excel Tips for Accountants Webinar": Launch Price: £49 - £79 per attendee.
      • Example: "3-Hour Hands-On Xero Efficiency Workshop": Launch Price: £99 - £149 per attendee (if in-person/interactive online).
    • Consider offering a "first session free" or heavily discounted pilot to a small group to gather feedback and testimonials.
  4. Target Initial Audience: Existing Clients & Local Network (Target: Week 2-3):
    • Identify finance staff within Phipps Henson McAllister's existing SME clients who would benefit from the specific initial training topic.
    • Reach out to other local (Northampton/Midlands) accounting firms or sole practitioners who might value this specific upskilling for themselves or their staff. Leverage any existing professional relationships.
  5. Create Simple Promotional Material & Announce First Session(s) (Target: Week 3):
    • Draft a clear email invitation and/or a simple flyer (PDF) for the first training session(s).
    • Clearly state: Topic, What You'll Learn (key outcomes), Date/Time/Format, Price, and How to Register.
    • Highlight that the training is delivered by professionals from Phipps Henson McAllister who combine tech expertise with deep accounting understanding.
    • Circulate to the identified target audience (existing clients, local network). Use LinkedIn for broader, yet targeted, visibility within the local accounting community.
  6. Deliver First Training Session(s) & Actively Solicit Feedback (Target: Week 4-5):
    • Conduct the initial training session(s). Focus on engagement and practical value.
    • At the end of the session, provide a simple feedback form (digital or paper) asking about content relevance, delivery clarity, perceived value, and suggestions for improvement. Also ask for testimonials if participants are happy.
    • This feedback is invaluable for refining the topic, materials, delivery style, and pricing for future sessions.
  7. Refine & Plan Next Steps Based on Feedback (Target: Post-First Session):
    • Analyze the feedback. What worked well? What could be improved?
    • If the first session was successful, schedule further dates and promote it more widely using testimonials.
    • Consider what other topics, based on your team's core expertise and initial feedback, could be developed next using this lean launch model.
    • Begin exploring CPD accreditation for popular, refined workshops to add further value.
