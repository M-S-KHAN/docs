App Advisory Services

A Strategic Service Line for Phipps Henson McAllister

1. Introduction

In today's digitally-driven business environment, Small and Medium-sized Enterprises (SMEs) are increasingly reliant on a diverse array of software applications to manage their finances, operations, and customer relationships. However, navigating the vast "app ecosystem" to select, implement, and integrate the right tools can be overwhelming and time-consuming.

App Advisory Services address this critical need by providing expert guidance to businesses, helping them leverage technology to streamline processes, enhance efficiency, and unlock growth. For an accountancy firm like ours, with a robust team of software engineers possessing deep accountancy domain knowledge, offering App Advisory is a natural extension of our capabilities. It allows us to provide holistic, tech-forward solutions to our existing client base and attract new clients seeking to optimize their business operations.

This service focuses on understanding client needs, recommending tailored app stacks (e.g., for accounting, payroll, CRM, inventory), assisting with setup and integration, and providing ongoing support and optimization.

2. SWOT Analysis

Strengths Weaknesses
  • Dual Expertise: Unique combination of senior software engineering skills and in-depth accountancy domain knowledge within the team.
  • Trusted Advisor Status: Existing strong relationships and trust with a diverse client base (SMEs).
  • Understanding of SME Pain Points: Deep familiarity with the financial and operational challenges faced by SMEs.
  • Direct Client Access: Ability to readily introduce the service to existing accounting, payroll, and advisory clients.
  • UK-Based Team: Local understanding of UK business practices and regulations (e.g., MTD).
  • New Service Perception: As a new service line, initial brand recognition for specific tech advisory might need to be built.
  • Diverse App Knowledge: Ensuring consistent, deep knowledge across the team for an ever-expanding array of business apps requires ongoing effort.
  • Resource Allocation: Balancing existing software development tasks with new advisory service delivery.
Opportunities Threats
  • High Market Demand: Significant need among UK SMEs for guidance on app selection and integration. Sage research indicates 92% of UK SMBs see tech as vital but often hesitate due to cost and uncertainty.
  • Growing App Ecosystem: Constant emergence of new and improved apps creates ongoing advisory needs.
  • Increased Client "Stickiness": Offering integrated tech solutions can deepen client relationships and reduce churn.
  • Revenue Growth: Xero research showed firms using multiple integrated apps saw 81% report revenue growth, and "app-master" firms credited their app stack for profit increases.
  • Cross-Selling: App advisory can lead to other service opportunities (e.g., custom automation, data analytics).
  • Competitive Differentiation: Positioning the firm as a modern, tech-forward accountancy practice. The research identified competitors like Linford Grey and TaxKings actively promoting such services.
  • Competition: Other accountancy firms and IT consultants are also entering the app advisory space.
  • Rapid Technological Change: Apps and platforms evolve quickly, requiring continuous learning and adaptation.
  • Client Cost Sensitivity: Some SMEs may be hesitant to invest in advisory services, requiring clear ROI demonstration.
  • Vendor Lock-in Concerns: Clients may worry about becoming too dependent on a specific app ecosystem.
  • Maintaining Objectivity: Ensuring recommendations are genuinely best for the client, avoiding bias towards specific partnered apps.

Our team of senior software engineers already possesses a strong foundation in technology and accounting principles. The primary learning curve involves specializing in the business application ecosystem relevant to SMEs:

  • Platform Expertise:
    • Achieving certifications like Xero Certified Advisor (approx. 6-8 hours online) and QuickBooks ProAdvisor.
    • Gaining deep familiarity with Sage Business Cloud Accounting partner programs and features.
  • App Ecosystem Knowledge:
    • Familiarization with popular integrated apps for key functions:
      • Receipts & Data Capture: Dext (formerly Receipt Bank), AutoEntry, Hubdoc.
      • Accounts Receivable/Credit Control: Chaser, Satago.
      • Reporting & Forecasting: Futrli, Fathom, Float.
      • Payroll: BrightPay, KeyPay, Sage Payroll.
      • CRM, Inventory, Payments: Integrations with platforms like Shopify, Stripe, Cin7.
    • Understanding the strengths, weaknesses, pricing, and integration capabilities of these apps.
  • Integration Platforms:
    • Basic understanding of tools like Zapier and Microsoft Power Automate for connecting apps where native integrations are lacking or insufficient.
  • Advisory & Commercial Skills:
    • Developing frameworks for client needs assessment in the context of app selection.
    • Effectively evaluating and communicating the ROI of different app stacks.
    • Understanding change management principles to help clients adopt new tools.

Effort Estimation: Initial ramp-up to cover core platforms and a selection of key apps might take a few weeks of focused effort per team member (including certifications). However, this is primarily about familiarization and systemization of knowledge. Continuous learning (e.g., attending vendor webinars, exploring new apps) will be an ongoing requirement (e.g., a few hours per month).

4. Ease of Launch / "Quick Win" Potential

App Advisory Services are identified as a TOP PRIORITY for achieving "Quick Wins" and generating revenue rapidly.

  • Low Barrier to Entry: The service leverages many existing skills (accounting knowledge, general tech-savviness, client communication). The additional specific app knowledge is acquirable relatively quickly.
  • Immediate Applicability to Existing Clients: We can approach our current diverse SME client base who are likely already using or considering various business apps. This provides a warm market for initial offerings.
  • Simple Initial Offerings: "Quick win" services can include:
    • "App Health Checks" or "App Stack Reviews."
    • Assistance with basic setup and configuration of core accounting apps and key integrations (e.g., bank feeds, receipt capture).
  • Rapid Certification: Key platform certifications (Xero, QuickBooks) are accessible online and can be completed swiftly.
  • Demonstrable Value: The benefits of well-chosen and integrated apps (time savings, efficiency) can be quickly demonstrated to clients. Research shows firms using multiple integrated apps saw 81% report revenue growth and 82% saving significant time on manual tasks.

5. Pricing Strategy

A flexible pricing strategy is recommended to cater to different client needs and engagement scopes for App Advisory Services:

  • Hourly Consulting:
    • For ad-hoc advice, troubleshooting, or smaller scopes of work.
    • Suggested Rate: £50 - £100 per hour, based on UK market research for app advisory and accounting tech consulting.
  • Fixed-Price Packages (One-Time Fees):
    • Ideal for "quick win" introductory services and well-defined deliverables.
    • Example: "Cloud App Health Check / Stack Review": £250 - £500 (covering a review of current apps, identification of gaps/inefficiencies, and recommendations for 2-3 key improvements). This aligns with competitor research on similar "Cloud Health Check" offers.
    • Example: "New Xero/QuickBooks Setup & Basic Integration": £400 - £800 (covering company setup, chart of accounts, bank feed connection, and one key app integration like Dext).
  • Project-Based Fees:
    • For more extensive implementation projects, such as migrating to a new accounting system plus multiple app integrations.
    • Pricing would be based on a detailed scope of work, estimated hours, and complexity. Fees could range from £1,000 to £5,000+.
  • Bundled / Retainer Services:
    • App advisory could be incorporated as a value-added component within existing monthly accounting or advisory retainers for key clients.
    • An ongoing "App Optimization & Support" retainer could be offered post-implementation (e.g., £100 - £300 per month for ongoing checks, updates, and minor support).

Note: Initial launch offers should be priced competitively to encourage uptake and gather testimonials. Clearly define the scope of each pricing model in engagement letters to manage client expectations, as highlighted in risk mitigation strategies for scope creep.

6. Steps to Launch & Secure First Customer

To rapidly move from current state to landing our first App Advisory client, focusing on a "quick win" approach:

  1. Fast-Track Team Readiness (Target: 1 Week):
    • Designate 1-2 software engineers with strong accounting understanding to lead the initial App Advisory charge.
    • Prioritize and complete foundational certifications: Xero Certified Advisor and QuickBooks ProAdvisor. These are often free, online, and can be achieved within a few days (6-8 hours each as per research).
    • Undertake rapid, focused familiarization (e.g., demos, key feature reviews) with 3-5 core SME apps identified in the research (e.g., Dext/AutoEntry for data capture, a popular UK payroll app like BrightPay, and a simple cash flow tool like Float or Futrli).
    • Develop a V1 internal checklist/playbook for conducting a basic "SME App Health Check" – this ensures a consistent approach even for initial engagements.
  2. Define & Price Specific "Quick Win" Launch Offers (Target: Concurrent with Step 1):
    • Based on market research (e.g., "Cloud Health Check" offerings), create 1-2 highly specific, low-commitment introductory packages. Examples:
      • "SME App Efficiency Audit": A 2-hour consultation to review current app usage, identify 1-2 key pain points, and provide 3 actionable recommendations. Launch Price: £199 - £249.
      • "Xero/QBO Optimisation Starter": A 3-hour service to review an existing Xero/QBO setup, ensure bank feeds are optimal, and integrate one core data capture app (e.g., Dext). Launch Price: £299 - £399.
    • Ensure pricing is attractive for an initial offering, aiming for ease of client acceptance.
  3. Identify & Prioritize Initial Prospects from Existing Client Base (Target: Days 3-5):
    • Systematically review Phipps Henson McAllister's existing SME client list.
    • Identify 5-10 clients who:
      • Have previously inquired about tech solutions or expressed frustration with manual processes.
      • Are known to be using cloud accounting but perhaps not optimally.
      • Represent good, communicative relationships ("friendlies") who would be open to piloting a new service and providing honest feedback.
    • This leverages our trusted advisor status and direct client access.
  4. Create Minimal Viable Marketing Collateral (Target: Days 4-6):
    • Develop a concise one-page PDF flyer or a well-crafted email template for the defined "Quick Win" offer(s).
    • Clearly state the service, the specific deliverables, the launch price, and the key benefits (e.g., "Save X hours per week on data entry," "Gain clearer financial insights," "Ensure your apps are working together seamlessly"). Reference impactful statistics from research like "90% less data-entry time" or Xero’s findings on "81% revenue growth" for app-savvy firms.
    • Highlight Phipps Henson McAllister's unique selling proposition: expert software engineers who understand accounting.
  5. Direct Outreach & Soft Launch to Existing Clients (Target: Week 2 onwards):
    • Begin approaching the prioritized list of existing clients.
    • Introduce the new service during routine review meetings, via a personalized email, or a direct call. Frame it as: "We're launching a new App Advisory service designed to help businesses like yours get the most out of their technology. As a valued client, we'd like to offer you our [Quick Win Offer Name] at a special introductory rate."
    • Focus on a consultative approach, listening to their current challenges before positioning the solution.
    • Aim to secure 1-3 initial "pilot" clients who agree to the service.
  6. Activate Immediate Low-Cost External Visibility (Target: Concurrent with client outreach):
    • Ensure Phipps Henson McAllister is promptly registered on the Xero Advisor Directory and QuickBooks ProAdvisor platform. As research confirms, these are free and can generate inbound leads.
    • Identify one relevant local (Northampton/Midlands) SME networking event or an online forum (e.g., AccountingWEB, local business Facebook groups) where the new service can be briefly mentioned or an introductory post shared.
  7. Execute First Engagements & Systematically Collect Feedback (Target: Weeks 3-4):
    • Deliver the "Quick Win" service to the first clients using the V1 playbook.
    • Crucially, implement a structured way to gather detailed feedback on their experience, the value received, the pricing, and any suggestions for improvement. This feedback is vital for refining the service offering for broader launch.
    • Capture any positive outcomes as potential (anonymized if necessary) testimonials or mini-case studies ("Client A saved X hours after our App Efficiency Audit").
