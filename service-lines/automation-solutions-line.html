Custom Automation Solutions for Accounting Processes

A Strategic Service Line for Phipps Henson McAllister

1. Introduction

Accounting and finance departments in many UK businesses, particularly SMEs and even within accounting practices themselves, are often burdened by repetitive, time-consuming manual tasks. These include data entry, invoice processing, bank reconciliations, report generation, and payroll administration. Such manual processes are not only inefficient but also prone to errors, hindering productivity and diverting skilled personnel from higher-value strategic activities.

Custom Automation Solutions offer a powerful remedy by developing and implementing bespoke software solutions (scripts, bots, integrations) tailored to automate these specific accounting workflows. For Phipps Henson McAllister, with its team of senior software engineers who possess significant accountancy domain knowledge, this service line represents a prime opportunity to deliver transformative value. We can design and build automation that goes beyond the capabilities of standard off-the-shelf software, directly addressing unique client pain points.

This service focuses on analyzing existing processes, identifying automation opportunities, and developing robust, efficient, and compliant automated solutions that integrate seamlessly with clients' existing systems.

2. SWOT Analysis

Strengths Weaknesses
  • Unmatched Dual Expertise: Deep software engineering capabilities combined with practical accountancy domain knowledge, allowing for nuanced and effective automation design.
  • Problem-Solving Focus: Ability to understand the 'why' behind accounting processes, leading to more intelligent automation.
  • Customization Capability: Can build truly bespoke solutions where off-the-shelf tools fall short or are too generic.
  • Existing Client Base: Potential to identify automation needs within current SME clients and other accounting firms we serve.
  • Credibility: As an accountancy firm, solutions will inherently be designed with compliance and financial best practices in mind.
  • Perception of Cost: Custom solutions might be perceived as more expensive than off-the-shelf software, requiring clear ROI demonstration.
  • Solution Maintenance: Custom automations can require ongoing maintenance, especially if underlying systems or regulations change (e.g., "bots break").
  • Sales Cycle: Convincing clients of the value of custom automation may involve a longer sales and education cycle than simpler services.
  • Scalability of Bespoke Solutions: Each solution is custom; scaling delivery requires careful project management and potentially team expansion.
Opportunities Threats
  • High Demand for Efficiency: Research shows 44% of UK businesses plan to adopt AI in finance/accounting, and over half of CFOs cite automation to relieve repetitive admin.
  • Significant ROI Potential for Clients: Automation can lead to dramatic time savings, error reduction, and cost savings (e.g., research citing 90% less data-entry time after automation).
  • Underserved Niche: Many SMEs have specific needs not met by large RPA vendors, creating a niche for tailored, smaller-scale automation.
  • Investment in Automation: An ICAEW survey found 71% of mid-tier managing partners intend to invest in AI/automation within 3 years, and 48% foresee rising client demand.
  • Value-Based Pricing: Potential for premium pricing based on the significant value and ROI delivered.
  • Long-Term Partnerships: Successful automation projects can lead to ongoing maintenance, support, and further development work.
  • Competition: Specialist RPA consultants, freelance automation experts (e.g., Zapier/Make.com consultants), and larger IT consultancies.
  • Rapid Evolution of Automation Tools: Off-the-shelf tools are constantly improving, potentially reducing the need for custom solutions in some areas.
  • Scope Creep: Custom development projects are notoriously prone to scope creep if not managed tightly.
  • Client Technical Debt: Clients' outdated existing systems can make custom integration complex and costly.
  • Resistance to Change: Staff may resist automation due to fear of job displacement or discomfort with new processes.

3. Learning Curve for Team

Our senior software engineers already possess strong programming, system design, and problem-solving skills, alongside accounting domain knowledge. The additional learning will focus on specific automation technologies and deeper integration patterns:

  • Robotic Process Automation (RPA) & Workflow Tools:
    • Training on platforms like Microsoft Power Automate (including RPA capabilities like PL-500 certification), UiPath, or Automation Anywhere if targeting more complex UI automation. For simpler workflows, proficiency in Zapier or Make.com.
    • Understanding best practices for designing, developing, and deploying software bots.
  • Integration & API Development:
    • Deepening expertise in working with APIs of common accounting software (Xero, QuickBooks, Sage) and other business systems (CRMs, e-commerce platforms).
    • Mastery of data formats (CSV, XML, JSON), API authentication (OAuth2, API keys), and webhooks.
  • Advanced Scripting & Programming for Automation:
    • Leveraging Python (with libraries like Pandas, Selenium), JavaScript (for web automation or Google Apps Script), or C#/.NET for building custom automation scripts and components.
    • Techniques for automating desktop applications or web UIs where APIs are unavailable.
  • Cloud Platforms & DevOps for Automation:
    • Utilizing cloud services (Azure Logic Apps, AWS Lambda/Step Functions) for hosting and managing serverless automation workflows or integration components.
    • Understanding of databases (for staging or logging data) and ETL (Extract, Transform, Load) processes if moving significant financial data.
  • Niche UK Accounting Rules & Process Nuances:
    • While already possessing domain knowledge, further deepening understanding of specific rules (e.g., complex VAT scenarios, MTD intricacies for specific industries, payroll regulations) is vital to ensure automations are fully compliant and robust.

Effort Estimation: Research suggests around 1-3 months of targeted training (e.g., a Microsoft PL-500 course is typically 3-5 days intensive, UiPath Developer Certification 20-40 hours study) plus practical on-the-job projects to become proficient in one major RPA/workflow platform and relevant integration techniques. Given our team's existing software engineering background, this would focus on the specific tooling and automation design patterns.

4. Ease of Launch / "Quick Win" Potential

Custom Automation Solutions can be a "Quick Win" IF strategically scoped to small, well-defined projects addressing clear client pain points.

  • Leveraging Existing Skills: Our core software engineering and accounting knowledge forms a strong base.
  • Targeted Small Projects: Instead of large-scale RPA deployments, focus initially on:
    • Automating specific, highly repetitive data entry tasks from structured sources (e.g., specific spreadsheets into accounting software).
    • Creating scripts for custom report consolidation or generation not easily done in standard software.
    • Building simple integrations between two key systems using tools like Zapier/Power Automate where our engineers can quickly configure and extend.
  • High Impact for Clients: Even small automations can deliver significant time savings and error reduction, quickly demonstrating value.
  • Phased Approach:
    • Phase 1 (Quick Win): Focus on script-based automation, advanced Excel/VBA, and connector tools (Zapier, Power Automate basic flows). These require less specialized RPA platform training.
    • Phase 2: Introduce more complex RPA solutions after building initial success stories and deeper team expertise in platforms like UiPath or Power Automate RPA.

Not a Quick Win If: Targeting large, complex, multi-system enterprise-grade RPA projects from day one. These require extensive discovery, development, and specialized tooling expertise that takes longer to establish.

5. Pricing Strategy

Pricing for Custom Automation Solutions should reflect the value delivered, complexity, and development effort:

  • Hourly Consulting & Development Rates:
    • For discovery, process analysis, custom scripting, and smaller automation builds.
    • Suggested Rate: £50 - £90 per hour for software development, potentially blended. RPA specialist contractors in the UK command £400-£600/day (approx. £50-£75/hr). Our combined expertise could justify rates in the upper end of the developer range or slightly above for specialized automation work.
  • Fixed-Price Project Fees (for well-scoped projects):
    • Ideal for defined "quick win" automation tasks or small to medium-sized projects.
    • Example "Quick Win" Project: "Automated Report Pack Generator": £1,500 - £5,000 (depending on complexity and number of data sources/reports).
    • Example: "Automated Invoice Data Entry from Specific Supplier Format": £2,000 - £7,000.
    • Research indicates small automation projects can range from £5k - £20k+. Initial "quick win" projects should aim for the lower end of this or slightly below to build traction.
  • Phased Project Billing:
    • For larger or more complex automation projects, break them into phases (e.g., Discovery & Design, Development & Testing, Deployment & Training) with separate pricing for each phase.
  • Retainer for Ongoing Support & Maintenance:
    • Post-implementation, offer a monthly or annual retainer for monitoring, updating, and maintaining the custom automation solutions.
    • E.g., £1k - £5k/month for support on more complex solutions, or a percentage of the initial project fee annually (e.g., 10-20%). For smaller automations, a lower fixed monthly fee.

Note: Emphasize the Return on Investment (ROI) by quantifying time savings, error reduction, and potential cost savings for the client. Value-based pricing arguments can be very strong for automation.

6. Steps to Launch & Secure First Customer

To launch Custom Automation Solutions, focusing on small, scoped projects for "quick wins":

  1. Internal Skill Sharpening & Tool Selection (Target: 1-2 Weeks):
    • Designate 1-2 software engineers with the strongest client-facing and process analysis skills to spearhead this.
    • Conduct a rapid internal review of accessible automation tools:
      • Ensure proficiency in Python scripting for data manipulation/automation.
      • Master a readily available workflow tool like Microsoft Power Automate (focus on cloud flows initially) or Zapier for simpler integrations. This aligns with a phased approach before deep RPA platform dives.
      • Review API documentation for Xero, QuickBooks, and Sage to identify common automation opportunities.
    • Develop a V1 "Process Automation Discovery Checklist" to use with potential clients to identify high-impact, low-complexity opportunities.
  2. Define & Package Initial "Quick Win" Automation Offers (Target: Concurrent with Step 1):
    • Identify 2-3 specific, common accounting pain points that are highly amenable to simple automation. Examples:
      • "Automated Monthly Report Consolidation": Takes data from 2-3 specific spreadsheets/sources and generates a consistent monthly report. Fixed Price: £950 - £1,950.
      • "Streamlined Data Import from [Specific System/Format]": Automates importing data from a regularly received file (e.g., sales from an e-commerce platform CSV) into the accounting system. Fixed Price: £1,200 - £2,500.
      • "Automated Reminder Email for Overdue Invoices": A simple script or flow that integrates with accounting software to send templated reminders. Fixed Price: £750 - £1,500.
    • Ensure these initial offers have a very clear scope and demonstrable ROI (e.g., "Saves X hours/month currently spent on manual report creation").
  3. Proactive Identification within Existing Client Base (Target: Week 2):
    • During regular client interactions (especially for App Advisory or accounting services), actively listen for mentions of repetitive tasks, data entry bottlenecks, or reconciliation frustrations.
    • Review your firm’s understanding of existing clients' processes to pinpoint those with obvious, simple automation potential. Focus on clients who are generally tech-receptive.
    • Select 3-5 existing clients who seem like strong candidates for a small, pilot automation project.
  4. Develop Targeted Communication & Proposal Snippets (Target: Week 2):
    • Create concise, benefit-driven descriptions for your "Quick Win" automation packages.
    • Prepare a simple proposal template that outlines: The Problem We Solve, Our Proposed Automated Solution (high-level), Key Benefits (Time Saved, Errors Reduced), Fixed Price, and What's Included/Excluded.
    • Highlight ROI statistics where possible (e.g., "Automating invoice capture can reduce data entry time by up to 90%").
  5. Introduce Automation Concepts & Pilot Offers to Selected Clients (Target: Week 3 onwards):
    • Approach the selected existing clients. Instead of a hard sell, offer a "Free Automation Opportunity Assessment" (using your V1 Discovery Checklist).
    • If a viable "quick win" project is identified, present the relevant fixed-price package as a pilot. Emphasize it as a way for them to experience the benefits of automation with low risk.
    • Example framing: "We've noticed you spend considerable time on X. We're launching a service to automate such tasks and believe we can build a solution for Y price that will save you Z hours. Would you be interested in being one of our first clients for this?"
  6. Execute Pilot Project(s) with Rigorous Documentation & Feedback (Target: Weeks 4-6):
    • For the first 1-2 clients, deliver the scoped automation solution. Maintain close communication throughout.
    • Thoroughly document the existing process, the developed solution, and the outcomes (time saved, errors reduced). This forms the basis of your first case studies.
    • Solicit detailed feedback on the process, the solution's effectiveness, and the perceived value. This is critical for refining your offering and pricing for future clients.
  7. Leverage Success for Broader Marketing (Target: Post-Pilot):
    • Once a successful pilot is completed, create an anonymized case study.
    • Begin to more broadly market the specific, proven "quick win" automation solutions to your wider client base and through channels identified in general market research (e.g., LinkedIn, accounting forums, partnerships with other firms lacking this capability).
