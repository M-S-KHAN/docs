What is Dropshipping? A Lean Approach to Retail

Dropshipping is an e-commerce fulfillment model where PHM would operate an online store without holding any physical inventory. When a customer places an order, PHM forwards it to a third-party supplier (often a manufacturer or wholesaler) who then ships the product directly to the customer. PHM acts as the storefront and customer relations manager, earning profit on the margin between the supplier's price and the retail price.

  • Low Capital Outlay: No need to invest in inventory, warehousing, or complex logistics.
  • Wide Product Selection: Ability to offer a diverse range of products without pre-purchasing stock.
  • Location Independence: Operations can be managed from anywhere with an internet connection.
  • Scalability: Order fulfillment is handled by suppliers, allowing for easier scaling of sales volume.

Phase 1: Foundation & Setup Critical Path (Days 1-7)

This initial phase is paramount for establishing the legal and financial groundwork. Decisions made here directly impact liability, tax, and operational clarity. Speed is essential, but not at the expense of proper structure.

1.1 Legal & Company Structure Critical

TASK: Form Separate Limited Company
Timeline: Days 1-2 (Initiate Immediately)
  • Incorporate a new private limited company online via Companies House (£12, ~24 hours).
  • Principals of PHM to own shares directly (sister company structure).
  • Rationale: Crucial for liability protection and brand separation.
  • If minor delays, can *briefly* operate under a trading name of PHM, but shift all operations to new Co. ASAP. Ensure trading name disclosures are correct if this route is taken temporarily.
Justification: As highlighted in the feasibility assessment, this step is fundamental to "ring-fence the accountancy practice from any risks of the retail trade" (n-accounting.co.uk). It also allows for independent branding and clear financial segregation.
TASK: Secure Business Name & Branding Assets
Timeline: Days 1-3
  • Register domain name (.co.uk and/or .com) for the new brand.
  • Perform a quick trademark search for the chosen brand name. Consider filing if unique.
  • Create a simple logo (Canva, Fiverr, or internal).
Justification: Establishes the online identity and initial brand presence, crucial for customer recognition and perceived professionalism. A trademark search mitigates IP risks.
TASK: Open Business Bank Account
Timeline: Days 3-7 (As soon as Co. is incorporated)
  • Open a dedicated business bank account for the new limited company.
  • Consider fintech banks (Starling, Tide, Revolut Business) for speed.
Justification: Essential for financial separation, accurate accounting, and maintaining the "limited liability veil." Fintechs offer rapid setup, aligning with the 30-day goal.

1.2 Tax & Initial Registrations Critical

TASK: Corporation Tax Registration
Timeline: Automatic upon incorporation (UTR sent by HMRC)
  • New company will be automatically registered for CT. Note UTR when received.
  • Be aware of "associated companies" rules for CT thresholds (att.org.uk).
Justification: A mandatory legal step. Understanding associated company rules immediately impacts tax planning for the new entity alongside PHM.
TASK: VAT Registration & EORI Number
Timeline: Days 3-7 (Apply concurrently)
  • Decision Point:
    • If importing goods from outside UK (e.g., China): Register for VAT from Day 1 to comply with low-value import VAT rules (gov.uk). This is highly probable for most dropshipping.
    • If sourcing 100% from UK suppliers AND turnover expected < £90k: Defer VAT registration, but monitor closely.
  • Apply for a GB EORI number (free, online via HMRC) – essential if importing.
Justification: Critical for compliance with UK import VAT rules on low-value goods, which often necessitates immediate VAT registration for dropshippers. An EORI number is mandatory for customs. PHM's expertise can ensure this is handled correctly from the outset.
TASK: Initial Accounting Setup
Timeline: Days 5-7
  • Set up new company in accounting software (Xero, QuickBooks, etc. - leverage PHM expertise).
  • Establish chart of accounts suitable for e-commerce (sales, COGS, marketing, platform fees, etc.).
  • Enable bank feeds once account is open.
Justification: Leverages PHM's core competency for accurate financial tracking from day one, crucial for performance measurement and tax reporting.

Phase 2: Platform, Product & Marketing Setup (Days 5-15)

With the legal foundations in place, this phase focuses on building the operational core: the online store, product sourcing, and preparing for customer acquisition.

2.1 E-commerce Platform & Supplier Integration High

TASK: Select & Set Up E-commerce Platform
Timeline: Days 5-10
  • Recommended: Shopify (ease of use, app ecosystem, quick setup).
  • Sign up for a plan (utilize free trial initially).
  • Configure basic store settings, theme, and connect domain.
Justification: Shopify offers the fastest route to a professional online store with robust dropshipping integrations, minimizing technical barriers and speeding up time-to-market.
TASK: Install Dropshipping & Automation Apps
Timeline: Days 7-12
  • Install apps like DSers (for AliExpress), Spocket/Avasam (for UK/EU suppliers), or similar.
  • Integrate with Shopify for product sourcing and order automation.
Justification: Automation is key to a lean operation, reducing manual effort in order processing and inventory management, which is vital for scalability.
TASK: Set Up Payment Gateways
Timeline: Days 7-12
  • Activate Shopify Payments (Stripe) and PayPal Business.
  • Ensure linked to the new company's business bank account.
  • Complete all verification steps.
  • Test transactions.
Justification: Enables secure and trusted payment processing, accommodating customer preferences and ensuring revenue collection.

2.2 Product Selection & Listing Medium

TASK: Niche & Product Research
Timeline: Days 5-10
  • Identify a promising niche (e.g., Home Wellness, Gadget Gifts, Pet Accessories, Eco-friendly).
  • Research trending products (Google Trends, TikTok, Amazon Bestsellers, supplier platforms). Focus on items with good margins, proven demand, and reasonable shipping times.
  • Aim for 3-5 initial products, including one potential "hero" product.
Justification: Product selection is critical for early sales. Focusing on trending items in viable niches increases the probability of quick wins.
TASK: Source Products & Add to Store
Timeline: Days 10-15
  • Use chosen dropshipping apps to import selected products into Shopify.
  • Write compelling, benefit-driven product descriptions and use high-quality images.
  • Set pricing to achieve ~50%+ gross margin after COGS.
  • Clearly state estimated delivery times per product.
Justification: High-quality listings improve conversion rates. Transparent pricing and shipping information build customer trust.

2.3 Website & Initial Marketing Setup Medium

TASK: Finalize Website Content & Legal Pages
Timeline: Days 10-15
  • Create essential pages: About Us, Contact Us, FAQ.
  • Generate and customize: Privacy Policy, Terms of Service, Shipping Policy, Returns Policy. Ensure compliance with UK GDPR and Consumer Rights Act (14-day cancellation right).
  • Display full new company details (Name, No., Reg. Office, VAT No. if applicable).
Justification: Legally required and builds customer trust. Shopify templates provide a good starting point, to be reviewed by PHM for accuracy.
TASK: Set Up Marketing & Analytics Tools
Timeline: Days 12-15
  • Install Google Analytics and Facebook Pixel on the Shopify store.
  • Set up a Facebook Business Page and Instagram Profile for the brand.
  • Create accounts for Facebook Ads Manager and Google Ads.
Justification: Essential for tracking performance, understanding customer behavior, and enabling effective digital advertising from day one.

Phase 3: Launch & Initial Operations (Days 16-30)

The culmination of preparation: launching the store, initiating marketing efforts, and managing the first wave of orders and customer interactions. Flexibility and responsiveness are key during this period.

3.1 Go-Live & Initial Marketing Push High

TASK: Final Pre-Launch Checks & Go-Live
Timeline: Day 15-16
  • Thoroughly test the entire customer journey: browsing, cart, checkout, payment.
  • Verify order forwarding to supplier and email notifications.
  • Officially launch the store.
Justification: Ensures a smooth launch and positive first impression for early customers.
TASK: Launch Initial Marketing Campaigns
Timeline: Days 16-30
  • Start with a small, focused ad budget (e.g., £20-£50/day) on Facebook/Instagram.
  • Consider Google Shopping Ads for relevant products.
  • Offer a launch promotion (e.g., 10% off).
  • Post organically on brand's social media.
Justification: Drives initial traffic and sales. A small, controlled budget allows for testing and optimization.

3.2 Operations & Customer Service High

TASK: Order Fulfillment & Monitoring
Timeline: Days 16-30 (Ongoing)
  • Monitor incoming orders daily.
  • Ensure orders are processed by suppliers promptly.
  • Track shipments and update customers.
  • Manage cash flow for supplier payments.
Justification: Efficient fulfillment is key to customer satisfaction in dropshipping. Proactive monitoring prevents issues.
TASK: Customer Service Setup & Management
Timeline: Days 16-30 (Ongoing)
  • Set up a dedicated customer support email.
  • Respond to inquiries within 24 hours.
  • Handle returns/refunds according to store policy and consumer rights.
Justification: Good customer service builds trust and can differentiate the new brand, even with longer dropshipping delivery times.

Phase 4: Compliance, Review & Iteration (Ongoing from Day 1)

Beyond the initial launch, sustained success requires ongoing attention to legal and financial compliance, diligent performance monitoring, and a willingness to adapt based on results.

4.1 Insurance & Ongoing Legal Compliance Medium

TASK: Obtain Necessary Insurance
Timeline: Secure before first sale (Aim for Days 10-15)
  • Essential: Product Liability Insurance (e.g., £1M-£2M cover). Disclose dropshipping model.
  • Consider Public Liability Insurance.
  • If employing staff for the newco: Employers’ Liability Insurance.
Justification: Protects the new company from significant financial risk associated with selling products.
TASK: ICO Registration
Timeline: Within first month of operation
  • Register the new company with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) as a data controller.
Justification: A legal requirement for handling customer personal data under UK GDPR.
TASK: Ongoing Tax Compliance
Timeline: Ongoing
  • Maintain accurate bookkeeping. File VAT returns quarterly (if registered).
  • Prepare for annual accounts and CT600 submission.
Justification: Fundamental for legal operation and accurate financial assessment, leveraging PHM's strengths.

4.2 Performance Monitoring & Optimization Medium

TASK: Monitor Key Metrics & Ad Performance
Timeline: Daily from launch (Days 16-30+)
  • Track website traffic, conversion rates, sales (Shopify Analytics, Google Analytics).
  • Monitor ad spend, CTR, CPA, ROAS.
Justification: Data-driven insights are crucial for optimizing marketing spend and improving conversion rates.
TASK: Review & Iterate (Day 15 & Day 30 Checkpoints)
Timeline: Specific review on Day 15 and Day 30
  • Day 15 Review: Assess initial sales, traffic, feedback. Reallocate marketing/effort to what's working.
  • Day 30 Review: Calculate revenue, expenses, profit/loss. Decide next steps (scale, cut, pivot).
Justification: Formal review points ensure agile decision-making and allow for course correction based on early performance.

  • Profitability Focus: Continuously monitor margins and ad spend.
  • Supplier Reliability: This is paramount. If a supplier underperforms, find an alternative.
